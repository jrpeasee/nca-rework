11-12

Our 11-12 groups participate in various stages of competitive swimming, from novice swimmers to Junior Olympic champions. This group has several programs within every workout, each catering to the athletes' needs. Swimmers are expected to attend a minimum of 3 workouts each week. In addition to all NCA team functions, swimmers are highly encouraged to attend all appropriate meets. A dryland component is incorporated twice a week. 3 days/week: Monday, Wednesday, Friday @ 7:00-8:00pm Coach: Coach Amy 5 days/week: Monday, Wednesday, Friday @ 5:30-7:00pm | Tuesday, Thursday @ 5:00-7:00pm Coaches: Coach Nate & Coach Mickey & Coach Rob Equipment Needed: Fins, snorkel and pullbuoy required, coaches suggest water bottle, team cap, and mesh bag (go to team store) * if you have old equipment, ie: Speedo Biosfuse Fins, you don't need to buy new ones, just when you are making a new purchase.

13-14

The 13-14 program participates in various stages of competitive swimming, from novice level to Sectional qualifiers. This group has several programs within every workout, each catering to the athletes' needs, with a dryland component twice a week. Our staff carefully structure their practice group to maximize the potential of each swimmer in the water.Swimmers are required to attend a minimum of 3 workouts each week. In addition to all NCA team functions, swimmers are required to attend all appropriate meets. 6 days/week: Monday, Wednesday, Friday @ 3:00-4:30pm | Tuesday, Thursday @ 3:00-5:00pm | Saturday TBD Coach: Coach Nate & Coach Rob Equipment Needed: Fins, snorkel, pullbuoy and full hand paddles required, coaches suggest water bottle, team cap, and mesh bag (go to team store) * if you have old equipment, ie: Speedo Biosfuse Fins, you don't need to buy new ones, just when you are making a new purchase.

Senior III

The Senior III group is designed to develop and maintain technical refinement in all four competitive strokes. Although competing in this group is not mandatory it is highly encouraged. Most swimmers in this program will represent their high school in competition. There is no minimum attendance requirement as many swimmers in this group are multi-sport athletes and/or have different extra-circular activities. 6 days/week: Monday - Friday @ 7:00-8:30pm Coach: Coach Amy Equipment Needed: - Finis front mount snorkel/Speedo Equivalent - Speedo Power Paddles/Stroke Makers (smallest) - Z2 Zoomers - Mesh Bag - Optional: Kick Board (store page) * if you have old equipment, ie: Speedo Biosfuse Fins, you don't need to buy new ones, just when you are making a new purchase.

Senior II

The Senior II group is designed to help athletes mature physically and behaviourally as succesful student-athletes and ultimately collegiate swimminmers. Swimmers in this program are required to attend 5 out of 6 weekly workouts and compete regularly. Competition is based on ability and this group attends local, regional and national level competitions. 6 days/week: Monday - Friday @ 7:00-8:30pm | Saturday TBD Coach: Coach Mickey Equipment Needed: Snorkel, Z2 Fins or Biofuse, Paddles, Pullbouy, mesh bag, team cap, water bottle (go to team store) * if you have old equipment, ie: Speedo Biosfuse Fins, you don't need to buy new ones, just when you are making a new purchase.

Senior I

Coaches: Coach Jeff & Coach Mickey
Practice Schedule: 3:00-5:30pm, 6 - 9 workouts per week for 90-180 minutes
Qualifications: Must have proven to the coaches a devotion to training and competition as well as a skill level appropriate to the current Senior group. Must be in the Pre Senior Program for a minimum of 3 months or have Coaches recommendation to be "fast tracked" to Senior group.
Goals: Senior swimmers must commit to a very demanding and intensified training program that includes morning workouts and a comprehensive dryland program. Senior swimmers prepare for local, regional, national and international competitions and ultimately the attainment of a college swimming opportunity.
Equipment Needed: Snorkel, Z2 Fins or Biofuse, Paddles, Pullbouy, mesh bag, team cap, water bottle (go to team store)

NCA Boosters

The NCA Booster's is made up of North Coast Aquatics parents. The NCA Boosters Board is comprised of a President, Past President, Vice President, Treasurer, Secretary, five members at large and Head Coach Jeff Pease. The primary function of the NCA Boosters is to raise money to support swimmers and coaches. The fund-raising dollars go to support team travel and to provide for awards, championship t-shirts & caps, educational clinics & seminars, equipment, swim fee scholarships and team socials.

Major events and activities that the NCA Boosters have sponsored are: Holiday party, JO t-shirts and caps, Annual Awards presentation (helps to subsidize), Relay event, Coaches education, Needs at sites - bins for equipment, pace clocks, kick boards, etc., Amazing Quest and other swimmer incentives, Swimmer of the Month awards

The above activities and events are organized and hosted by NCA Boosters. They take a great deal of work and organization from not only the NCA Booster Board, but from all the parents and families who's swimmers are members Message from the North Coast Aquatics Boosters Did you know that Individual and Corporate Donations to the North Coast Aquatics Boosters are tax deductible? Below are examples of how NCA Team Members have taken advantage of this:

  • Donate on an individual basis.
  • Donate through your employer-sponsored United Way/CHAD campaign by designating the North Coast Aquatics Boosters as the "qualified" charitable organization.
  • Donate on an Individual basis (employee) and have donation matched by company (employer).
  • Solicit a donation from a company that sponsors youth sports organizations.

The North Coast Aquatics Boosters' goal is to provide financial assistance through fund-raising to benefit team travel, the Kay Pentz scholarship fund for college-bound athletes, a scholarship program for swimmers who experience financial hardships, awards, and team equipment and functions.
