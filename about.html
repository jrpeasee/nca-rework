About



North Coast Aquatics is the largest competitive swim team in San Diego County. Over the past 34 years NCA has helped place over 240 of its swimmers into major colleges across the country, and has been the highest achieving swim club in San Diego County for the last 2 decades.

North Coast Aquatics offers a complete program dedicated to excellence, from the novice to world ranked and Olympic class swimmer. Our swimmers have won international gold medals, including Kurt Grote, Olympic gold medal (1996) Pan-Pacific gold medal (1997) and World Championships gold medal (1998), and Tim Siciliano, Pan American gold medal (1999). NCA swimmers have swum in competitions throughout the world in such places as Australia, Canada, Sweden, France, Japan, China, Italy, Mexico, Serbia, and Brazil.

NCA has placed several of its 18-under swimmers on to the USA Swimming National Jr Team and won medals in international competitions around the world. This includes recent NCA alums Eric Hedlin (2013 World Championships silver medal) and Kendyl Stewart (2012 Jr Pan Pacifics gold medal). In addition, 8 NCA swimmers qualified for the 2008 USA Olympic Trials (including top 8 finalist Tanya Krisman) and 10 NCA swimmers qualified for the 2012 Olympic Trials (including a repeat top 8 finish by Tanya Krisman). Due to the success of NCA swimmers, North Coast Aquatics was awarded Gold Medal Status by USA Swimming from 2008-2012 as one of the top 20 club teams in the United States.

History



Head Coach Jeff Pease founded North Coast Aquatics in 1979 as one of the first coach owned and operated swimming programs in California. Starting in a community homeowners pool in Encinitas California, NCA struggled in the early years with limited facilities but established its reputation as "quality not quantity" by producing elite swimmers from the beginning.

NCA moved into the City of Carlsbad's first swim complex in 1982, allowing Coach Pease the opportunity to "step up" the program and provide more opportunity for a greater number of swimmers. While growing, NCA always kept the swimmer to coach ratio at workable levels to ensure quality athlete development.In 1992 NCA expanded to the Carlsbad Boys & Girls Club and in 1995 added a major site in La Jolla, CA at the Jewish Community Center. In 2014 NCA moved most of it's Carlsbad programs to the new Alga Norte Pool and is now a program of over 550 swimmers with a staff of 10 professional coaches under Coach Pease's direction. Coach Pease hired Head Age Group Coach, Sue Parnes, in 1999 and Coach Parnes has been instrumental in guiding NCA into the 21st century.

Head Coach Jeff Pease has been on the USA National Team Coaching staff since 1997 and been a head coach or assistant coach for several USA Swimming National Team teams that competed in Sidney Australia (2003 and 2005), Melbourne, Australia (2007) and Barcelona, Spain (2010). In Addition, Coach Pease has been awarded the Certificate of Excellence by the American Swim Coaches Association as one of the top producing club coaches in the United States since 2006.

Philosophy



North Coast is committed to the long term development of their swimmers and places the highest value on teamwork, citizenship, academics and character building. The NCA teaching methodology is based on skill development first and training second. The North Coast way leads a youngster through carefully graduated stages as their physical development unfolds and their mental readiness grows. NCA swimmers form lifelong friendships with their teammates and competitors. We hope that your stay with our team, however short or long term, will be enjoyable and rewarding.
